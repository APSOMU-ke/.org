About APSOMU

The Association of Political Science Students of Moi University (APSOMU) is a student-led organization dedicated to empowering Political Science scholars through academic enrichment, professional development, and community engagement.

Founded to address the pressing questions and aspirations of students, APSOMU provides a platform where learners can explore the discipline beyond the classroom, connect with practitioners, and prepare for impactful careers in governance, policy, and public service.

Stakeholders of our Association

1. Students

Students are the main stakeholders, APSOMU is primarily focused on promoting and advancing study of political science among students.

2. Scholars

Association provides opportunities for students to engage with different scholars and practitioners therefore this individuas are stakeholders of the organization.

3. Higher Education

Association works to promote research and scholarships in the field of political science and to influence policy and contribute to the development of political science as a discipline. Therefore higher institutions are stakeholders.

4. Government

How APSOMU is represented in national arena

1. Representation through Student Leadership Structures

APSOMU can channel its voice through the Moi University Students Organisation, which officially represents students at institutional and sometimes national levels.

Through MUSO, student issues can reach university Senate and national education stakeholders.

This gives APSOMU indirect influence on national student policies.

2. Participation in National Conferences & Forums

Members can attend:

  • Political science conventions
  • Youth leadership forums
  • Kenya Model United Nations (KMUN, etc.)

Such platforms expose APSOMU to national dialogue, policy debates and networking with other universities.

3. Collaboration with Other Universities' Associations

APSOMU can partner with similar associations in institutions like University of Nairobi Students Association.

Inter-university collaboration helps:

  • Share ideas
  • Build a unified student voice nationally
  • Organize joint academic or advocacy events

4. Engagement in National Policy and Civic Activities

As a political science body, APSOMU can:

  • Participate in public participation forums
  • Engage county assemblies and national government discussions
  • Advocate on governance, democracy, and youth inclusion

This strengthens its identity as a policy-influencing student body.

5. Media and Public Intellectual Presence

APSOMU can gain national visibility by:

  • Writing articles, blogs, or policy briefs
  • Engaging media discussions on political issues

This positions the association as a thought leader among youth.

6. Networking with National Umbrella Student Bodies

Just like organizations such as Association of Muslims in Universities and Colleges in Kenya that unite students nationally, APSOMU can:

  • Join or initiate national political science student networks
  • Advocate collectively for academic and professional interests

7. Alumni and Professional Linkages

APSOMU can leverage alumni from Moi University working in:

  • Government
  • NGOs
  • International organizations

This builds a national footprint and influence.

8. Hosting National Events

APSOMU can position itself by organizing:

  • National political science symposiums
  • Public lectures (like inviting national figures such as PLO Lumumba)

This attracts attention from across Kenya and elevates the association's profile.
